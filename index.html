About

Curiosity as a compass

I'm Ibtisam Toure, a Computer Science student with a deep focus on Artificial Intelligence. I'm drawn not only to how intelligent systems function — but to the larger questions they raise about human thinking, meaning, and identity.

My technical work sits alongside a genuine interest in psychology and the philosophy of technology. I believe the engineers building AI systems have a responsibility to understand what they are reshaping — not just technically, but humanly.

I write reflective essays about these themes. My piece  The Spiritual Cost of Artificial Intelligence ↗  explores how technological progress can quietly erode human meaning and belief systems — a question I think the field rarely takes seriously enough.

I believe the future of AI should be guided not only by engineering excellence, but by ethical awareness and a deep understanding of what makes us human.
